January 22nd, 2006: A Poem for Doc Clark

There’s snow on the north tower this morning. There wasn't any snow on the north tower last night, but now there is.

There’s snow on the trees and there’s snow on the grass and snow floating on icicles down the Elwha River. There’s snow on the bears and there’s snow on the snowshoe hares and there’s snow on the little otter family that sometimes eats its dinner of salmon. There’s snow on the line where the grass and trees meet the water. There’s snow on the marmots that burrow underground, but even they pop their heads up and get snow on them. There’s snow by the door to my room and a little bit of snow that has peaked under the door. There is even a speck of snow on my blanket, since a tiny bit of the snow flew under the fireplace and lofted its way to me, as if attracted to the only source of biological warmth, narrowly evading the radiator and the radio and the computer.

But, there is no snow on me. There is no snow on me because I am wearing a shirt and underwear and pants and a sweater and a sleeping bag and two to three blankets on top of my skin. There is no snow on me because I am wearing many layers.

There is no snow on the south tower.

The snowstorm laid a bed on all of the park. I can clear a path to my binoculars and look at all of Olympic and not see a single tree without any snow on it. I can look at all of Olympic and not see a single mountain goat walking around without a little snow on its little head. I can look at all of Olympic and even the hawks and the eagles and the sprouses have snow on them as they make their winter migrations.

But there is no snow on the south tower.

But there is no snow on the south tower.

yabadabadoo
