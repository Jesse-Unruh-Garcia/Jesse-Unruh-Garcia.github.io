Clubmoss, Dicranum, Sphagnum, Liverwort, Hylocomniums, Rhizominiums, Clamaciums, Plagiothecium, Fontinalises. Sometimes they overflow so heavily that they feel like they are the trees themselves. In many ways they are, as many of the mosses have a symbiotic relationship with the trees, providing moisture and nitrogen in exchange for a place to call home. Exchanging life for life, nurture for nurture, nature growing with nature in a marriage of green and brown.

It feels cold and wet in the winters, but hydrating. Like sucking on a sponge. It feels soft and natural and I press my hands against the walls and watch it make an imprint. My imprint. A green mirror of my hand. A wave from another side of me.

But the imprints bounce back over time. I know, I’ve checked. Until recently.



The meteor crashed in the Hall of Moss. The moss began to burn, flex, and form. They created a million hand prints. All over, every inch of the moss impressioned. And all my perfect reflection. Sometimes the rabbits slept in the palms of the moss, sometimes the goats ate from them. I couldn’t believe it at first, the way I could hold it. It hugged me, it held me closer. I had left a real impression. I was there, the forest remembered me when I never did.

Doc Clark says the meteor caused a fight or flight response from the moss. As a result, their cell structures communicated to each other to revert to their last impressioned form. He says it's merely coincidence that it happens to be my hand. I bet he thinks Mary just happened to be pregnant too. I’m not saying I’m Jesus, oh no no.
