Break Through

The broken branches of the trees continue to grow. Breathing. Reshaping and resurfacing new forms. Attempting new combinations of flowers on the fly. All of them exotic to Olympic. Orchids and lilies and irises. Doc Clark and I grabbed as many samples as we could and took them back to the tower. Doc Clark can’t explain this one, and I thought he was supposed to be some sort of big shot. What a dunce. But I think I get it.

It’s like a river. Rivers go through the path of least resistance. The misconception most people have is that this always works. But that’s not true. Broken rivers make tributaries, streams, lakes. But most dry up, most never fulfill their true form. But the river doesn’t stop just because of one wrong move. Rivers are forces beyond nature, they shape nature. They plow through trees and upend mountains with the right force and the right amount of time. That’s what the branches are doing.

They’ve been broken, misshapen by something cosmic and eldritch and beyond understanding. But life is beyond nature, they shape nature. And so the branches are attempting each tributary of life, until something sticks. Maybe it’s a flower, maybe it’s just a bud.

But I will keep trying. I will keep flowing. I will grab the branch and let it fuse with my fingers and fuse with my skin and let daisies grow on my back and let orchids grow on my eyeballs. I will break through.

I am beyond nature, I shape nature. I am what I wish for and I am the wish.
