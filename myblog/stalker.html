Stalker

Before he died, Henry’s favorite movies were always art school hocus pocus. Like Solaris and Paris, Texas and Eternal Sunshine. I would call him a grandiloquent and he would laugh because that’s not a word that real people use.

Stalker was Henry’s favorite movie. A stalker is a guide, someone who knows the nature of something unnatural. Knows all its tricks. Knows how to evade them. Henry said I was his stalker in the park, leading him around. Dodging goats and pitfalls and poisonous plants. He said he was Andrei Tarkovsky, writing a part of my story. I called him pretentious for it. He’s still pretentious, but he’s also dead so it doesn’t matter.

Now I feel as if I really am a stalker. Walking through the park, through the crash site, observing the way nature changes and learning to traverse it. Combing through clues, finding my way to the journey’s center. But who am I leading? Myself? Doc Clark? Henry? The goats and the hares and the moss?

Or am I just Henry’s fungi, eating up all his flashiness.


Endings

At the end of the movie, the stalker makes his way to the Wish Granter. But it’s a monkey's paw, and it grants the user's truest desire, not their wish. They tell a story of a stalker who wished his brother would be saved, but becomes wealthy instead. And so the stalker does not make a wish, out of fear that it reveals their coldness, their selfishness, that at their truest form they are not as good as they wish to be.

I fear what I wish for.
