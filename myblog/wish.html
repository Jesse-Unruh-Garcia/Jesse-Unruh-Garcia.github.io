My Zone, My Wish

After months of study, Doc Clark left the surveying of the crash site to me. I had filled books about the changing plants and the moss and the hiding goats and the curious hares. And I hadn’t forgotten. I had slowed, yes. I had broken trees, yes. I had shaped nature.

But I still hadn’t forgotten.

I was still a stalker, returning to the center of my zone. But I had never made a wish. Not yet. I buried Henry’s ashes under the dirt. I felt something come over, something primordial and alien and eldritch. Whatever I wanted, it would be granted to me. I sat at the center on my knees and wondered about what it would be.
