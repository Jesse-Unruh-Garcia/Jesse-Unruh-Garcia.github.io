The End.

I told Doc Clark I was ready to leave Olympic. I’ve done all I could study. I’ve observed the new and the strange. I’ve cried rivers and have flowed them through mountains.


The hares crowded around me. They held me and they stained me and they left an imprint where there never was. They spread their flowers, and I accepted them with open arms. They reminded me of the kindness that life brings.

The goats have accepted my resignation letter. Their legs have begun to shrink, and they now sit much lower to the ground. But they can kick harder than ever. They reminded me to reject isolation.

The moss has bounced back. They no longer show my reflection, my ghost. They are greener than before, and as the snow begins to melt, they suck in its moisture. They reminded me that I’m remembered.

The flowers linger. Most won’t last long, they aren’t native. But some species will stick around and grow and assimilate to their changed environment. They remind me to push.

Every little thing he did. Every little life.




I have buried his ashes and can see a sapling form. I will watch it grow, I will nurture it, but I will learn to live apart from it.

I am still a stalker, but I have followed my river down its new path. It will erode mountains and cause cyclones and nurture fish and algae and the new and the strange.
