The Experiment
January 23rd, 2006:

Doc Clark believes in therapeutic expression. He’s not a therapist but he wants to be mine. Or a father. Or a brother. But usually he’s just my boss.

When it first happened he told me to spend a week in the south tower to germinate it. Feel it. Focus on the ice in my heart and by doing so help it melt. But then I started eating meals with the goats. So he decided blogging in the north tower was probably a smarter plan.

Blogging is the new hotness. The heat that can break glaciers.

I’m not always so sad. I’m usually a funny duckling! Doc Clark says I’m a silly goose. Henry used to say I was a total quack. I can still be a rambunctious critter, but it’s harder.



January 25th, 2006:

My parents named me Muir Davids.
John Muir wrote The Yosemite.
Henry David Thoreau wrote Walden.
I've written neither, not yet at least.

Everest (that’s Doc Clark’s first name) wrote a letter to me two weeks ago asking for me to come back to the park. He likes having me around and he likes when I do my job instead of moping around Port Angeles. But, I like moping.


He said: “ok but can you mope in the North Tower.”
I said: “errrrrrghhhhh.”
He said: “I’ll let you bring a computer.”
I said: “ehhhhhhhhhh.”
He said: “I’ll let you write a blog.”
I said: “hmmmmmmm.”
He said: “I’ll let you drink on the weekends.”
I said: “deal.”

We Killed Me
Henry died alone while I sat in the south tower. I passed with him. Doc Clark sent me to the North Tower for revival.
