January 24th, 2006: Tripping

The good thing about Winters in the park is that they’re quiet. Way too damn cold for the tourists to be crowding around the corners. Only people I see are the old married couple from Port Angeles. SO damn cute with their matching beanies, I think they must’ve knitted them together. Holding hands and limping along, true love might really last forever. Fuck them!

Anyways, note that the Snowshoe Hares don’t mind me anymore. Or the tower. They’re like little road bumps now. Tripped on one this morning, it’s tiny little squeal when my face planted into the snow was heartbreaking. I got on all fours and started apologizing like crazy. “I’m sorry! I’m sorry! I never meant to hurt you little one!” But it ran off before I could really get any message across.

I think I’m going to leave out a little bowl of daisies so that the bunnies can eat them while I’m not around. My penance for not looking close enough at the ground. Fall to my knees and beg that the bunny-god doesn’t kill me in my sleep. Watch the cloud’s break as a giant eldritch bunny-lord hovers over me and makes me apologize, like my mom after I broke the old tea pot.
